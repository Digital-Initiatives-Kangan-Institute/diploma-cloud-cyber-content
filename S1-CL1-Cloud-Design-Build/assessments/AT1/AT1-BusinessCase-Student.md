YAT College is a Registered Training Organisation (RTO) based at 175 Cremorne Street, Cremorne VIC. YAT's ICT staff are highly capable on their current environment but lack cloud experience, which is why MTS has been engaged.

You are an **MP Tech Solutions (MTS) consultant** reporting to **Pat Lin** (MTS Senior Consultant). Pat liaises with **Sam Walker** (YAT ICT Manager) for sign-offs. Sam will receive your Business Case and is your primary YAT-side stakeholder.

AT1 is the **analysis and planning** phase of the engagement. The work you produce in AT1 flows into AT2 and AT3 of this cluster — the action plan approved at the end of AT1 becomes the brief for the build work in AT2.

## Part A — Business Case

Using the **YAT Business Case template** (download from the intranet's **Templates** section), populate every section. The Business Case is the primary written deliverable and must contain:

- **§1 Executive Summary** — a one-page summary of your recommendation (write last)
- **§2 Engagement context** — who you are, the engagement, the decision being asked of the board
- **§3 Strategic Alignment Analysis** — analyse YAT's ICT Strategic Plan against the industry environment and organisational objectives
- **§4 Current State of YAT's ICT** — synthesised summary from the intranet materials (synthesis, not verbatim reproduction)
- **§5 Gap Analysis** — gaps, improvement opportunities, proposed changes
- **§6 Options Considered and Evaluation** — workload definition, options assessed (in-house renewal vs cloud migration to AWS), initial impact and difficulty assessment
- **§7 Cost-Benefit Analysis** — assumptions, summary tables for both options, avoided-downtime benefit, comparison summary, Year-1 cash-flow comparison, sensitivity analysis. The detailed line items live in Appendix 1 of the same document.
- **§8 Risk and Impact Assessment** — intangibles comparison and risk register for the recommended option
- **§9 Recommendation** — your chosen option with rationale
- **§10 Action Plan** — prioritised changes, implementation schedule, standards / targets / success metrics, implementation methods, alignment with YAT's change-management procedure, risk register
- **§11 Next Steps and Decision Requested** — what you are asking the board to decide today
- **Sign-off block** — completed during/after your presentation
- **Appendix 1 — CBA detailed line items** — every cost line you researched and computed
- **Appendix 2 — Knowledge Evidence** — short-answer responses linking your work to underlying cloud and evaluation principles
- **Appendix 3 — Supporting Research** — AWS Pricing Calculator export, citations
- **Appendix 4 — Feedback Record** — completed using the YAT Feedback Record template during/after the presentation

You will need to **research AWS pricing yourself** using the AWS Pricing Calculator (see Appendix 1 of the Business Case template for the structure your AWS line items must follow). YAT-internal cost figures for the on-prem option are provided in the scenario materials on the intranet.

## Part B — Presentation to the YAT board

Using the **YAT Board Presentation Deck template** (download from the intranet's **Templates** section), prepare a deck that walks the YAT board through your Business Case. The deck is **8–10 slides plus speaker notes**; each slide walks the board through one section of the Business Case.

Review at least one example of a previous board presentation deck from the YAT **Document Archive** on the intranet — these accompany the previous business cases referenced for Part A — as a reference for what a YAT board presentation looks like in style, depth, and length.

You will deliver the presentation to the **YAT board**, role-played by your assessor (as Sam Walker, YAT ICT Manager, and/or Pat Lin, MTS Senior Consultant) and a peer (as a board member).

### The presentation event

- **Duration:** 10–15 minutes of presenting + 5 minutes of board questions, feedback, and sign-off
- **Format:** in-person on campus or via video conference (confirm with your assessor)
- **Required artefacts at the event:**
  - The completed Business Case submitted to the board at least **48 hours in advance**
  - The deck with populated speaker notes
  - A printed or shared **Feedback Record** ready to be populated during/after the meeting (using the YAT Feedback Record template)
  - A printed or shared **sign-off sheet** for the board to sign at the end (the Sign-off block from the Business Case)

### During the event you will:

- Walk the board through your Business Case using the deck
- Answer the board's questions during Q&A (the assessor's questions probe both the analysis and your underlying knowledge of cloud and evaluation principles)
- Capture board feedback in the Feedback Record
- Obtain the board's sign-off on the action plan in Business Case §10

## Tips for success

- **Start with the scenario.** Read the YAT intranet end-to-end before you start writing — particularly the ICT Strategic Plan, the current ICT environment, the role brief, and the ICT manager consultation notes.
- **Synthesise, don't copy.** §4 (Current State) is the section most likely to drift into verbatim reproduction. Write it in your own words and focus on what's material to the migration.
- **Show your AWS pricing working.** Generate an AWS Pricing Calculator estimate, export it, and attach it as Appendix 3. Numbers without evidence are not credible.
- **Sense-check your CBA totals.** Year-1 outlay differences matter to senior management even when 5-year totals favour one option — make the cash-flow position visible.
- **Be honest in §8 Intangibles.** A one-sided intangibles table — only positives for your preferred option — is less credible than an honest trade-off discussion.
- **The action plan is what the board approves.** §10 is the most scrutinised section. Spend time on prioritisation, scheduling, and alignment with YAT's change-management procedure.
- **Prepare for the Q&A.** The assessor will probe both the analysis and your underlying knowledge of cloud and evaluation principles. The Q&A is when your speaker preparation pays off.
- **Bring the sign-off sheet to the meeting.** Sign-off is part of the assessment — you cannot achieve Satisfactory without it.
